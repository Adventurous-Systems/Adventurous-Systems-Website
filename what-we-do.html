Digital Twin Integration

Unifying design, construction, and operational data through Web3-enabled digital twins.

The Challenge

Construction projects generate fragmented data across disconnected systems — BIM models, project management platforms, IoT sensors, financial software. This fragmentation costs the industry £54 billion annually in rework, delays, and lost insights.

Our Approach

We design and implement digital twin architectures that create unified, queryable representations of built assets throughout their lifecycle. Our twins integrate:

  • Building Information Models (BIM/IFC)
  • Real-time IoT sensor data
  • Satellite and geospatial information
  • Blockchain verification layers
  • Operational and maintenance systems

Research-Validated Frameworks

Our methodology draws from published research in computational design and graph database theory, not vendor marketing.

Open Standards

We build on IFC, LBD (Linked Building Data), and open ontologies — no vendor lock-in.

Verifiable Provenance

Blockchain integration ensures every data point has cryptographically-verified history.

Outcomes

Single source of truth across stakeholders Automated compliance verification Predictive maintenance via pattern recognition Reduced rework through real-time coordination

Ideal For

  • Large infrastructure projects requiring multi-party coordination
  • Retrofit programmes needing verified carbon savings
  • Asset operators seeking integrated facility management
  • Organizations pursuing digital transformation strategies

Blockchain for Construction

Smart contracts and decentralised protocols that automate verification, enforce transparency, and enable circular material tracking.

The Challenge

Construction supply chains lack transparency. Material origins are unverifiable, carbon calculations are disputed, payments require manual reconciliation, and circular reuse systems can't scale without trusted provenance.

Material Passports

Blockchain-verified histories of materials from manufacturing through demolition.

Supply Chain Transparency

Traceable provenance for sustainability certification.

Smart Contracts

Automated payment on verified milestone completion.

Circular Economy Infrastructure

Reuse marketplaces with trusted material data.

Authority in This Space

We literally wrote the book: "Blockchain for Construction" (Springer, 2022) synthesizes five years of research and pilot implementations. Our work contributes to international standards development and has secured competitive research funding from Scottish Government and European programmes.

Outcomes

Verifiable sustainability claims Reduced manual reconciliation Enabled circular supply chains Trust through transparency

Ideal For

  • Material suppliers seeking differentiation through verified sustainability
  • Contractors managing complex supply chains
  • Circular economy initiatives requiring scalable trust infrastructure
  • Organizations facing ESG reporting requirements
