FREQUENTLY ASKED

Common Questions

What is a digital twin in construction?

A digital twin in construction is a dynamic, data-rich virtual representation of a physical built asset — a building, bridge, or infrastructure system. Unlike a static BIM model, a digital twin continuously integrates real-time data from IoT sensors, satellite imagery, maintenance systems, and operational records. This creates a "living" model that reflects the asset's actual state across its lifecycle, enabling predictive maintenance, automated compliance verification, and data-driven decision making. Our digital twins also incorporate blockchain verification layers for cryptographic provenance of every data point.

How is blockchain used in construction?

Blockchain provides a transparent, immutable ledger for construction data. Key applications include: material passports that track materials from manufacture through demolition with verified provenance; smart contracts that automate payment release when verified milestones are completed; supply chain transparency for sustainability certification and ESG reporting; and circular economy infrastructure enabling trusted reuse marketplaces. Our TRACE project, funded by the Scottish Government, demonstrates these capabilities in a live pilot connecting demolition contractors, reuse hubs, and builders.

What does an AI readiness assessment involve?

Our AI Readiness Assessment is a structured two-week engagement that evaluates your organisation across six dimensions: Data Infrastructure, Process Maturity, Technical Capability, Governance Frameworks, Use Case Viability, and Organisational Readiness. Week one involves stakeholder interviews, software ecosystem mapping, data flow analysis, and process documentation review. Week two delivers an AI Readiness Score, a priority roadmap with quick wins and strategic initiatives, ROI projections, and a 90-minute strategy session. Unlike generic IT assessments, ours is built specifically for AEC workflows and data structures.
